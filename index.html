Research interests

Bayesian model development, computation, and statistical inference; network meta-regression modeling for continuous (univariate and multivariate) and ordinal outcomes using aggregate and individual patient data; survival analysis for large-scale survey and observational studies; clinical trial design and statistical methodology; statistical methods for missing data; graphical and network-based modeling of brain connectivity; complex high-dimensional biomedical data (neuroimaging, genetics, EHR) and multimodal data integration.

Research experience

Yale University, Department of Biostatistics (2023–present)

Postdoctoral Associate with Prof. Yize Zhao.

  • Led large-scale neuroimaging studies (ABCD, HCP) on functional–structural gradient coupling and behavioral/genetic drivers.
  • Investigated structural connectivity mediation of genetic effects on functional brain networks.
  • Developed Bayesian hierarchical models for tau protein propagation in individualized functional connectivity.
  • Established semiparametric lifetime biomarker models with age/cohort/survival selection and kernel-profile estimating equations.
  • Developed Bayesian longitudinal network regression and supervised brain network construction methods.

Pfizer Research and Development (2020–2023)

Student Research Fellow with Dr. Steven Gilbert.

  • Reconstructed legacy SAS datasets for IBS trials; implemented prespecified analysis plans and subtype efficacy analyses.
  • Designed benchmarking procedures for reference-based multiple imputation (MAR, CR, J2R, CIR, LMCF) in R and SAS.

University of Connecticut, Department of Statistics (2019–2023)

Research Assistant with Prof. Ming-Hui Chen.

  • Analyzed beverage intake and risk of hypertension/hyperlipidemia with interval-censored survival and factor analysis models.
  • Implemented Bayesian network meta-regression for pharmaceutical collaborations with Merck, Amgen, and UConn Kinesiology.
